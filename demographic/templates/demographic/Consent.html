Purpose of
Research		 You are being asked to volunteer for this research study to help us better understand the formation and persistence of collective identity through a three-phase experiment. We will test hypotheses related to theories in collective identity with these experiments and compare the outcomes to our predictive models. We anticipate up to 4000 recruited participants per experiment. The researchers are faculty members and students at the following academic institutions: Virginia Tech, Stanford University, Cornell University, Northwestern University, and Johns Hopkins University.
Criteria for
Participation		 You must be at least 18 years of age to participate in this study. We are inviting you to participate in this research project, because you are at least 18 years of age, understand English, understand US currency, and use an Internet-enabled computer.
Procedures The experiment consists of a web application that is the main interface. The experiment will proceed as follows with an introduction and survey followed by three phases of the game. Throughout the game, visual and textual questions may be asked in the form of exit surveys in order to clarify various decisions made throughout the phases. The flow of the game will proceed as follows:
  1. Personal Survey. You will be asked for sociodemographic information. If you decide to leave the game, all of this information will be deleted automatically.
  2. Introduction. You will be introduced to the use of the game and how to interact with its various features.
  3. Understanding of game. Next, a web form will test for a basic understanding of the game that follows. After this, you will be randomly assigned to a team for the three-phase play of the game.
  4. Phase 1: Word Count Exercise. The screen will contain a set of letters that your team must work together on to produce a threshold (i.e., a minimum) number of words. The letters will consist of the American alphabet and the accepted words will be American English. You will be able to see your own words as well as those produced by other members of the team. This exercise will be timed. Fill out exit survey.
  5. Phase 2: Additional Earnings Opportunity. Having completed Phase 1, a new interface will be displayed where members of the group will have the option to contribute a portion of their winnings to the team "pot" of money. Contributions will be totaled and the redistributed to the team following a prescribed amount. Fill out exit survey.
  6. Phase 3: Group Competition. Your team will then be paired with another team. One team will receive an additional amount of money that they can choose to share with the other group. Members of the other team must determine a minimum level of sharing that they will accept. If the deal succeeds, both teams split the money in the agreed-upon way. A team splits any money evenly. Otherwise, no team succeeds, meaning neither team receives any money. You will be able to communicate with members of your team through chat interfaces. This session will be time limited to encourage discussion and each group member has to decide whether or not to make (or accept) the offer from members of the other group. Fill out exit survey.
  7. Conclusion: After the final phase, the final screen will display the results of the game and give the user's final winnings from participating. The game is now complete.
Time Required You will spend at most 2 hours in the session today. There is no obligation for any additional sessions. However, you may be asked to volunteer again in the future to participate in a similar experiment.
Risks There are no anticipated risks.
Benefits There will be no direct benefits to you by participating in this study, other than typical types of remuneration for these experiments. However, your participation will help us learn about various aspects of collective identity and group behavior. No promise or guarantee of benefits has been made to encourage you to participate.
Compensation There will be a $0.05 - show-up fee for agreeing to participate. Rewards in the subsequent phases will reflect the decisions made and will range from $1 - $5.
Voluntary
Participation		 Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may refuse to take part in the research or exit the study at any time without penalty. You are free to decline to answer any particular question or disengage from the game at any time for any reason without question.
Right to
Withdraw from
the Study		 You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without any negative consequences. Note: As per Amazon Mechanical Turk standard, you will earn rewards only for the tasks you appropriately complete before your withdrawal. There is no penalty for withdrawing, and you will still earn a $.05 show-up fee. Your worker ID will not be stored upon withdrawal.
Anonymity and
Confidentiality

Your individual privacy will be protected in all papers, books, talks, posts, or stories resulting from this study. In order to protect the confidentiality of your responses, we will assign you an identification (ID) number, and your decisions and the collected data will be recorded using that number, without a link to your name or other identifiable information. The ID number will be stored separately in a database which can only be accessed by authorized personnel.

Your name will not be used in any report, and we will obtain no information linking your name to the ID number. The results of the study, including laboratory or any other data, may be published for scientific purposes and may be shared with other researchers, but we will not give your name or any identifiable references to you.

Please be aware that any work performed on Amazon Mechanical Turk can potentially be linked to information about you on your Amazon public profile page, depending on the settings you have for your Amazon profile. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Amazon public profile page. We will not store your Mechanical Turk worker ID or any other personally identifiable information about you.

At no time will the researchers release identifiable results of the study to anyone other than individuals working on the project without your written consent. The Virginia Tech Institutional Review Board (IRB) may view the study’s data for auditing purposes. The IRB is responsible for the oversight of the protection of human subjects involved in research. Representatives from the Department of Defense (DoD) may view the study’s data for auditing purposes and protection of human subjects.
