Purpose of
Research		 You are being asked to volunteer for this research study to help us better understand how various team configurations function while playing different exercises. The researchers are faculty members and students at the following academic institutions: Virginia Tech, Stanford University, Cornell University, Northwestern University, and Johns Hopkins University.
Criteria for
Participation		 You must be at least 18 years of age to participate in this study. We are inviting you to participate in this research project, because you are at least 18 years of age, understand English, and use an Internet-enabled computer.
Procedures The experiment consists of a web application that is the main interface. The study will begin with a basic sociodemographic questionnaire and AMT usage habits. You will then be placed on a team. Your team will then participate in up to three exercises where you will interact through a game interface and submit decisions. Throughout the study you will be asked various survey questions. At the end of the study, you will be shown your final score and proceed back to AMT.
Time Required We expect the entire exercise will not take more than 30 minutes to complete.
Risks There are no anticipated risks.
Benefits There will be no direct benefits to you by participating in this study. However, your participation will help us learn about how individuals function in different team arrangements. No promise or guarantee of benefits has been made to encourage you to participate.
Compensation There will be a $0.05 - show-up fee for agreeing to participate. Rewards in the subsequent phases will reflect the decisions made and will range from $1 - $5 (we expect on average that a participant will earn $7.00/hr).
Voluntary
Participation		 Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You may refuse to take part in the research or exit the study at any time without penalty. You are free to decline to answer any particular question or disengage from the game at any time for any reason without question.
Right to
Withdraw from
the Study		 You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without any negative consequences. Note: As per Amazon Mechanical Turk standard, you will earn rewards only for the tasks you appropriately complete before your withdrawal. There is no penalty for withdrawing, and you will still earn a $.05 show-up fee. Your worker ID will not be stored upon withdrawal.
Anonymity and
Confidentiality

Your individual privacy will be protected in all papers, books, talks, posts, or stories resulting from this study. In order to protect the confidentiality of your responses, we will assign you an identification (ID) number, and your decisions and the collected data will be recorded using that number, without a link to your name or other identifiable information. The ID number will be stored separately in a database which can only be accessed by authorized personnel. The results of the study, including laboratory or any other data, may be published for scientific purposes and may be shared with other researchers, but we will not give your name or any identifiable references to you.

Please be aware that any work performed on Amazon Mechanical Turk can potentially be linked to information about you on your Amazon public profile page, depending on the settings you have for your Amazon profile. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Amazon public profile page. We will not store your Mechanical Turk worker ID or any other personally identifiable information about you.

At no time will the researchers release identifiable results of the study to anyone other than individuals working on the project without your written consent. The Virginia Tech Institutional Review Board (IRB) may view the study’s data for auditing purposes. The IRB is responsible for the oversight of the protection of human subjects involved in research. Representatives from the Department of Defense (DoD) may view the study’s data for auditing purposes and protection of human subjects.
